About Seven Company

A house of three disciplines,
built on one philosophy.

Founded in 2018, Seven Company specializes in affiliate marketing and digital advertising. We help global brands drive conversions through strategic partnerships and high-performance campaigns. The final destination is Freedom.

Our Story

From an affiliate experiment to a multi-discipline firm.

Seven Company began in 2018 when Dong Phong — running a series of solo affiliate marketing projects — discovered that careful media buying, strategic content, and the right partners could compound into something much bigger than any single campaign.

What started as a multi-channel affiliate network grew into a full performance-marketing practice managing more than $10M in ad spend across Google, Facebook, TikTok, Bing, and native channels — with an average campaign ROI of 350%.

As we worked alongside founders and investors, two adjacent needs kept coming up: where to put profits (real estate) and who to talk to about the next bet (community). So we built both — not as side projects, but as full disciplines led by senior people who could stand on their own.

How We Work

Six principles that guide every engagement.

1 · Partners, not customers

The phrase isn't decorative. It governs how we price, how we communicate, and how we behave when a campaign isn't working.

2 · Senior hands on the work

Founders and directors do the strategy and review every campaign. No layer of juniors learning on your spend.

3 · Measure the right thing

We define the number that matters before we spend a dollar of yours — and earn the right to celebrate only when it moves.

4 · Quiet about your business

Brands, deals, and people pass through our office every week. Confidentiality is reflex, not policy.

5 · Long over loud

We choose partners we expect to work with for a decade. Most of our top accounts started small and grew with us.

6 · The whole house

Marketing, real estate, and network advice from one team — strategies that reinforce each other instead of competing for budget.
